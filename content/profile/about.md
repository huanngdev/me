# About

The narrative anchor for the site. Source of truth for the About section, the SEO description, and the "elevator pitch" repeated across recruiter conversations.

## Anchor

**The one thing visitors should walk away knowing:** _Chris ships — fast, and to a finish._

Hackathons, side projects, deployed work — the proof is in things that exist, not credentials. The portfolio should feel like a shelf of finished work, not a list of intents.

## What lights him up

**Hard UI / interaction work.** Animations, complex state, polished components — the craft side of frontend. This is the thread that connects shadcn componentry, the lights-on/off avatar interaction on this site, the polish of his hackathon projects, and the kind of blog posts that would feel native here ("how I built X interaction").

Implication for projects: lean the showcase toward UI-heavy work. The "Components" section in CLAUDE.md is a perfect fit for this and should be prioritized.

## Origin

Self-taught from curiosity. Got into programming on his own — no degree story, no bootcamp story. The "curve, not the credentials" angle is real: from nothing to Sui hackathon wins inside a year.

This is a feature, not a footnote. Lead with it.

## Off-code

- **Gaming**
- **Music, anime, film**

Both are warm, low-cringe personality signals that translate well to international audiences. Useful for the about section's last sentence — humanizes the bio without feeling like filler.
